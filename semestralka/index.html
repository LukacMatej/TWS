Sekce 1

Jumping spiders are a fascinating family of spiders known for their agile and active hunting behavior. These spiders belong to the Salticidae family, which is one of the largest spider families, comprising over 6,000 species. Jumping spiders are found worldwide, with a diverse range of habitats, from tropical rainforests to deserts.

What sets jumping spiders apart is their excellent vision and remarkable jumping ability. They have large front-facing eyes, including two especially large ones, which give them binocular vision and depth perception. This keen eyesight helps them accurately judge distances and track prey.

Despite their small size, jumping spiders are formidable hunters. They use their silk for safety lines but do not construct webs to catch prey. Instead, they actively stalk and pounce on their victims, often leaping several times their body length to catch insects, other spiders, or even small vertebrates.

Sekce 2

Blue hair tarantulas, scientifically known as Chromatopelma cyaneopubescens, are striking arachnids native to tropical forests of northern Venezuela, Guyana, and Suriname. As their name suggests, they are characterized by their vibrant blue-colored hairs covering their bodies, which makes them a popular choice among tarantula enthusiasts.

These tarantulas typically have a leg span of around 4 to 5 inches when fully grown, with females being larger than males. Despite their intimidating appearance, blue hair tarantulas are relatively docile and are known for their calm temperament, making them suitable pets for experienced spider keepers.

In the wild, blue hair tarantulas inhabit trees and build silk-lined burrows or retreats where they can hide during the day and emerge at night to hunt for prey. Their diet primarily consists of insects, although they may occasionally consume small vertebrates.

Sekce 3

Rose hair tarantulas, scientifically known as Grammostola rosea, are one of the most popular tarantula species in the pet trade. Originating from the deserts and scrublands of Chile, these tarantulas have gained popularity due to their docile nature, ease of care, and striking appearance.

Adult rose hair tarantulas typically have a leg span of around 4 to 5 inches, with females being larger and heavier-bodied than males. They are named for the rosy-pink coloration on their abdomen and legs, which contrasts beautifully with their dark-colored carapace.

In the wild, rose hair tarantulas construct burrows or retreats in the soil, where they spend much of their time hidden during the day and emerge at night to hunt for prey. Their diet primarily consists of insects, although they may occasionally consume small vertebrates.While they possess urticating hairs for defense, they are less likely to flick them compared to other tarantula species.
